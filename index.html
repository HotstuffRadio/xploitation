Blog Post Day 3

For the first hour, I listen intently to ***, hanging onto his every word as he drones about ** and **, servers, customers, internet protocols, landing pages, log ins, error messages, covering every mundane detail under the sun. I find myself yawning appallingly, trying hard to cover my gaping mouth. ***’s voice is soothing; hush and velvety, like a lullaby. By the second hour, I’m dozing off and my mind is miles away in snooze land with my cats leaning against me. My mouth hangs open and drool seep out, Gently, I extricate myself from the drool monster and rub my temples. Oh God. How the hell am I supposed to survive through weeks and then a year or so of this mind numbing crap?.

Blog Post Day 4

Day 4 of creaks by. M, C, T and I sit in the same seats, and the four of us have developed an easygoing, relaxed sort of comradeship. In spite of myself, our trainer V has quickly grown on me. He can be a tad ironic at times, which is great. He’s dull, witty, and everything nice, with an extra heavy dose of humor. He's a deleted cast member of Greek Friends. I’ve come to understand why V is training teams. Surely as the rest of this is soooo dull. And C has been a huge help. She picks up all the training material in a snap, aces the troubleshooting exercises and blitzes through the tech. As for me, I barely scrape through. I hate the pressure of cramming everything in, and having to spit it all out at a moment’s notice, so sitting next to C + A has come in handy. Whenever something is too ‘beyond’ Gen X, all I have to do is turn to C, and she graciously obliges..
